Konrad

To learn about atmospheric processes and how they influence temperature, we can use climate models. Konrad is a single column model of the tropical atmosphere, from the surface upwards through the atmosphere. This kind of model is a 1D Radiative-Convective Equilibrium (RCE) model, because the main processes involved are

In models of the atmosphere, radiative transfer describes how light from the Sun and heat from the Earth travel through and interact with the atmosphere.

and convection. Many real world processes are missing from konrad, including cloud formation, winds and heat transport from the tropics to mid or high latitudes. Despite this, we can still use konrad to learn some interesting things about the tropical atmosphere. For example, we can study how the temperature depends on different gases, solar irradiance, the strength of convection, and the properties of clouds.

For a certain set of input parameters (the amounts of various gases, an initial temperature profile, surface properties, the amount of incoming solar radiation), we use a radiative transfer scheme to calculate radiative heating rates. These are applied to the input temperature profile, producing a new temperature profile. If the new temperature profile is unstable to convection, a convective adjustment is applied in the troposphere. This changes the temperature profile again, giving us another new temperature profile. Then we calculate changes in other atmospheric properties, such as the water vapour concentration. The new set of parameters is used as input to the radiative transfer scheme and the whole process is repeated many times. Eventually, the change to the temperature profile becomes very small, and we say that the system has reached equilibrium.

Konrad is written in python and is available on our github page for anyone to download and use. The radiative transfer scheme used in konrad is RRTMG, the development of which started in the 1990s, and the scheme continues to be used in many weather prediction and climate models. RRTMG is written in fortran and made accessible through python by climt.
