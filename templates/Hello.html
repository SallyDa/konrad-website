Konrad

Konrad is a single column model of the tropical atmosphere, from the surface upwards through the atmosphere. This kind of model is a 1D Radiative-Convective Equilibrium (RCE) model, because the main processes involved are radiative transfer and convection. Convection occurs in the troposphere, and causes cloud formation. In konrad, convection takes heat energy from the surface and brings it up into the troposphere, but it does not model cloud formation. Many real world processes are missing from konrad, including winds and heat transport from the tropics to mid or high latitudes. Despite this, we can still learn some interesting things about the real tropical atmosphere.

Konrad is written in python and is available on our github page for anyone to download and use. The radiative transfer scheme used in konrad is RRTMG, the development of which started in the 1990s, and the scheme continues to be used in many weather prediction and climate models. RRTMG is written in fortran and made accessible through python by climt.
