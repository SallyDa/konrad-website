Some background information

Atmospheric CO2 concentrations have been increasing strongly since pre-industrial times, when there were about 280 ppmv. CO2 is a greenhouse gas, which means that it absorbs and emits longwave radiation. This stops some of the radiation from escaping to space and causes the surface and troposphere to warm.

Water vapour is also a greenhouse gas, and as the atmosphere warms, it is expected that the amount of water vapour in the air will increase such that the relative humidity remains constant.
